180 steps per minute

Running music

The optimum running rhythm

The idea that 180 steps per minute is the most efficient rhythm for runners goes back to a study at the 1984 Olympics. Coach Jack Daniels' observation of middle and long distance runners found that all the elite athletes were running 180 steps per minute or more. Since then, a general consensus has emerged that shortening your strides and increasing their number will help you improve performance and avoid injury.

I put the theory to the test as a relative newcomer to running, increasing my rhythm from 140 to 180 steps per minute, and haven't looked back since. I can now run further, faster with less aching during and after.

To help me do that I went searching for 180 beat per minute rhythms and tried metronome clicks, Spotify and beat measuring apps with variable accuracy. So then I decided to start making music specifically for the purpose, combining an old love of writing music with a new one of running. These tracks are free for you to download or stream, and I'll keep adding more - so come back now and then or keep an eye on the feed or #180stepsperminute..... one thing that distinguishes these tracks from many of the other options I've written about is that they alone are guaranteed to be exactly 180 bpm!
